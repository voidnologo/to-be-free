# LDS Primary Sources: Religious Freedom, Liberty & the Constitution
### A Reference Compilation of Addresses by the First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve Apostles

---

> **Compiler's Note:** This document collects and correlates primary source addresses, speeches, and statements by members of the First Presidency and Quorum of the Twelve Apostles of The Church of Jesus Christ of Latter-day Saints on the topics of religious freedom, liberty, and the U.S. Constitution. It merges and reconciles two research passes (June 2026). Sources are drawn from official Church websites (`churchofjesuschrist.org`), the official Church Newsroom (`newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org`), and BYU Speeches (`speeches.byu.edu`). All links verified June 2026.
>
> **Important note on leadership:** President Russell M. Nelson passed away in 2025. President Dallin H. Oaks was sustained as the eighteenth President and Prophet of the Church in October 2025. References to Oaks before that date reflect his role as Apostle or First Counselor; after that date he serves as Church President.

## 1. Ezra Taft Benson

*Elder and later President of the Church (Apostle 1943–1994; President 1985–1994). Benson is the most prolific LDS voice on constitutional liberty and freedom, spanning five decades of addresses. He served as U.S. Secretary of Agriculture under President Eisenhower (1953–1961), giving his political commentary unique civic authority.*

---

**Title:** "The Proper Role of Government"  
**Author:** Elder Ezra Taft Benson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** October 1968 (General Conference address; published in *Improvement Era*, December 1968; also delivered as a BYU Forum, October 21, 1968)  
**Context:** One of Benson's most referenced treatises — a foundational statement on constitutionalism, limited government, and individual liberty. Draws heavily on Frederic Bastiat and the Founding Fathers. Argues that rights pre-exist government and that the state's sole legitimate role is to protect God-given freedoms. Often reprinted as a standalone pamphlet.  
**Link (BYU Speeches — audio):** https://speeches.byu.edu/talks/ezra-taft-benson/proper-role-government/  
**Link (Full text):** https://latterdayconservative.com/ezra-taft-benson/the-proper-role-of-government/

---

**Title:** "Civic Standards for the Faithful Saints"  
**Author:** Elder Ezra Taft Benson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** April 1972 General Conference  
**Context:** General Conference address. Benson identifies three "civic standards" for Latter-day Saints: (1) the inspired Constitution, (2) the scriptures (especially the Book of Mormon), and (3) the living prophets. Extensively quotes President J. Reuben Clark on allegiance to the Constitution. Warns against secret combinations seeking to overthrow freedom.  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1972/04/civic-standards-for-the-faithful-saints?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "God's Hand in Our Nation's History"  
**Author:** Elder Ezra Taft Benson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** March 28, 1977 (BYU Devotional)  
**Context:** BYU devotional address arguing against revisionist framings of the American founding. Benson refutes the notion that Founding Fathers merely borrowed Enlightenment ideas, pointing instead to divine providence and Benjamin Franklin's famous prayer-for-guidance moment at the Constitutional Convention. Ties the Constitution to the Restoration.  
**Link:** https://speeches.byu.edu/talks/ezra-taft-benson/gods-hand-nations-history/

---

**Title:** "God-Given Freedom: This Nation Shall Endure"  
**Author:** Elder Ezra Taft Benson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** December 4, 1973 (BYU Devotional)  
**Context:** BYU devotional. Argues the United States is "God-ordained for a glorious purpose." Includes President David O. McKay's London Temple dedicatory prayer on freedom as a divine gift, and President J. Reuben Clark's statement that the Constitution is "a great and treasured part of my religion." Calls Latter-day Saints to study the founding documents and defend constitutional principles.  
**Link:** https://speeches.byu.edu/talks/ezra-taft-benson/nation-shall-endure/

---

**Title:** "The Constitution — A Glorious Standard"  
**Author:** Elder Ezra Taft Benson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** April 1976 General Conference  
**Context:** General Conference address. One of Benson's landmark constitutionalism speeches. Argues for the Constitution's divine inspiration, pays tribute to the Founding Fathers, declares righteousness the "indispensable ingredient to liberty," and warns the nation risks losing its freedoms through sin. Quotes Abraham Lincoln and J. Reuben Clark extensively.  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1976/04/the-constitution-a-glorious-standard?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "Our Priceless Heritage"  
**Author:** Elder Ezra Taft Benson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** October 1976 General Conference  
**Context:** General Conference address. Honors both the Founding Fathers and Mormon pioneers as co-inheritors of a legacy of sacrificial liberty. Challenges Latter-day Saints to preserve their heritage.  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1976/10/our-priceless-heritage?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "A Witness and a Warning"  
**Author:** Elder Ezra Taft Benson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** October 1979 General Conference  
**Context:** General Conference address. Warns of communism as an existential threat to American freedom. Argues the U.S. is vulnerable because of lost faith in God. Calls for return to constitutional government and Christian principles. Quotes in full the 1936 First Presidency statement against Communism (signed by Grant, Clark, and McKay). Contains the oft-cited warning: "Once freedom is lost, only blood — human blood — will win it back."  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1979/10/a-witness-and-a-warning?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "The Constitution: A Heavenly Banner"  
**Author:** President Ezra Taft Benson, President of the Church  
**Date:** September 16, 1986 (BYU Devotional)  
**Context:** BYU devotional delivered as Church President. One of Benson's most thorough constitutional treatments: outlines five major constitutional provisions he considers crucial to freedom — sovereignty of the people, separation of powers, limited government, republicanism, and the Bill of Rights. Warns the Constitution has been chipped away for two centuries. Quotes Joseph Smith's prophecy about the Constitution one day being on "the brink of ruin." Delivered at the Constitution's bicentennial.  
**Link:** https://speeches.byu.edu/talks/ezra-taft-benson/constitution-heavenly-banner/

---

**Title:** "Our Divine Constitution"  
**Author:** President Ezra Taft Benson, President of the Church  
**Date:** October 1987 General Conference  
**Context:** General Conference address delivered as Church President, on the 200th anniversary of the Constitution. Argues God prepared the Founding Fathers as a divine prologue to the Restoration. Quotes 3 Nephi 21:4, 2 Nephi 10:11, and D&C 101. Calls Latter-day Saints to defend and uphold the Constitution.  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1987/10/our-divine-constitution?lang=eng

## 2. D. Todd Christofferson

*Elder, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles (called 2008); later Second Counselor in the First Presidency (2018–present). Former attorney. Has spoken extensively on religious liberty in both domestic and international contexts.*

---

**Title:** "A Celebration of Religious Freedom"  
**Author:** Elder D. Todd Christofferson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** April 29, 2015  
**Context:** Interfaith address at the Brazil Mosque in São Paulo — the first event of its kind in Brazil, attended by Muslims, Sikhs, Catholics, Jews, Evangelicals, and government leaders. Argues that religious freedom is the "cornerstone of peace" and essential to all other freedoms. Originally delivered in Portuguese. Republished in the *Ensign* (February 2018).  
**Link (full transcript):** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/a-celebration-of-religious-freedom  
**Link (Ensign version):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/ensign/2018/02/religious-freedom-cornerstone-of-peace?lang=eng  
**Link (news summary):** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/apostle-speaks-at-historic-religious-freedom-event-in-brazil

---

**Title:** "Religious Freedom — A Cherished Heritage to Defend" (Freedom Festival Patriotic Service)  
**Author:** Elder D. Todd Christofferson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** June 26, 2016  
**Context:** Keynote address at the America's Freedom Festival Patriotic Service, BYU Marriott Center, Provo, Utah. One of the most historically detailed treatments of American religious liberty by a modern LDS apostle. Reviews the colonial period, American Revolution, First Amendment jurisprudence, and current threats. Argues religious freedom is "woven into the very soul of America." Urges civic engagement.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/religious-freedom%E2%80%94a-cherished-heritage-to-defend

---

**Title:** "Religious Freedom — Protecting the Good Religion Does"  
**Author:** Elder D. Todd Christofferson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** September 27, 2018  
**Context:** Address at the G20 Interfaith Forum, Fourth Plenary Session, Buenos Aires, Argentina. Argues, quoting Lord Acton, that religious liberty is the "generating principle of civil liberty." Details how religious freedom enables charitable service, promotes civic virtue, and nurtures strong families. Argues that loss of religious freedom leads to erosion of all other freedoms.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/g20-interfaith-forum-transcript-elder-christofferson-religious-freedom

---

**Title:** "Religious Freedom and the Rule of Law" (Cambridge University)  
**Author:** Elder D. Todd Christofferson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** August 2017  
**Context:** Academic address at Cambridge University. One of the most legally sophisticated treatments of religious freedom by a Church leader, covering church autonomy, freedom of association, the Magna Carta, and the connection between religious liberty and the rule of law.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/transcript-elder-christofferson-cambridge-2017

---

**Title:** "Religious Liberty: The Basis of a Free and Just Society"  
**Author:** Elder D. Todd Christofferson, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** October 29, 2021  
**Context:** Address at the First Forum on Religious Freedom in the Southern Cone, Rio de Janeiro, Brazil. Argues that religious liberty promotes pluralism and peace, and that governments protecting it experience fewer social conflicts and greater cohesion. Cited in official Church curriculum.  
**Link (cited in curriculum):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/celebrating-freedom-and-agency/01?lang=eng

---

**Title:** Canterbury Medal Gala Invocation / Remarks on Religious Freedom, Philadelphia  
**Author:** President D. Todd Christofferson, Second Counselor in the First Presidency  
**Date:** June 11–12, 2026  
**Context:** President Christofferson offered the invocation at Becket's Canterbury Medal Gala at the National Constitution Center in Philadelphia, near Independence Hall, during the United States' 250th anniversary celebrations. Visited the Liberty Bell and Independence Hall. Praised Becket's work as focused on religious liberty "for everyone — not just certain faiths, but all faiths and even those who have no faith."  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/president-christofferson-philadelphia-toronto-religious-freedom

## 3. Quentin L. Cook

*Elder, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles (called 2007). Former attorney with extensive legal background. Has spoken on religious freedom in domestic and international contexts.*

---

**Title:** "Lamentations of Jeremiah: Beware of Bondage"  
**Author:** Elder Quentin L. Cook, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** October 2013 General Conference  
**Context:** General Conference address. Uses the lamentations of Jeremiah as a framework to warn against spiritual and political bondage in the latter days. One of the few General Conference addresses (other than Hales 2015 and Rasband 2022) specifically citing threats to religious freedom.  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2013/10/lamentations-of-jeremiah-beware-of-bondage?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "The Restoration of Morality and Religious Freedom"  
**Author:** Elder Quentin L. Cook, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** December 16, 2011  
**Context:** Commencement address at BYU–Idaho. Argues that restoration of public morality and protection of religious freedom are intertwined civic responsibilities. Challenges graduates to be examples of integrity. Condensed version published in *New Era* (October 2012) under the title "How to Defend Religious Freedom."  
**Link (full transcript):** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/the-restoration-of-morality-and-religious-freedom  
**Link (condensed — New Era):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/new-era/2012/10/how-to-defend-religious-freedom?lang=eng

---

**Title:** Religious Liberty Lecture, University of Notre Dame Australia School of Law  
**Author:** Elder Quentin L. Cook, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** May 27, 2015  
**Context:** Academic address at Notre Dame Australia's Sydney campus. Calls for a global interfaith coalition to defend religious liberty. Argues that protecting freedom of faith is the shared responsibility of all religions, not just one's own.  
**Link (news summary):** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/2015-year-in-review/article/elder-cook-calls-for-global-effort-to-protect-faith-and-religious-freedom

---

**Title:** Religious Freedom Address, Princeton University  
**Author:** Elder Quentin L. Cook, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** July 28, 2017  
**Context:** Academic address at Princeton. Features extended treatment of early LDS persecution in Missouri and Illinois as historical case studies in the necessity of protecting minority religious rights.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/transcript-%C2%A0elder-quentin-l-cook-speaks-on-religious-freedom-at-princeton-university

---

**Title:** "Tone Deaf to the Music of Faith" — Notre Dame Religious Liberty Summit  
**Author:** Elder Quentin L. Cook, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** June 28, 2021  
**Context:** Address at the first Notre Dame Religious Liberty Summit (Cardinal Timothy Dolan delivered the keynote). Cook lamented how many today are "tone deaf to the music of faith." Called for a cross-faith coalition — "Catholics, Evangelicals, Jews, Muslims, Latter-day Saints, and other faiths" — to defend religious liberty together.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/elder-cook-notre-dame-religious-freedom

## 4. Robert D. Hales

*Elder, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles (1994–2017). Delivered one of the only General Conference addresses focused exclusively on religious freedom in the modern era.*

---

**Title:** "Christian Courage: The Price of Discipleship"  
**Author:** Elder Robert D. Hales, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** October 2008 General Conference  
**Context:** General Conference address. Principally about discipleship and withstanding criticism of faith in public life; addresses the moral courage required to defend religious convictions in the public square.  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2008/10/christian-courage-the-price-of-discipleship?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "Preserving Agency, Protecting Religious Freedom"  
**Author:** Elder Robert D. Hales, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** April 2015 General Conference (Easter Sunday afternoon session)  
**Context:** General Conference address — among the most significant modern General Conference treatments of religious liberty. Delivered on Easter Sunday. Hales outlines four "cornerstones of religious freedom": (1) freedom to believe, (2) freedom to share one's faith, (3) freedom to form a religious organization, and (4) freedom to live one's faith publicly. Provides three practical steps for members. References Captain Moroni and the Title of Liberty. Closes with "Don't walk! Run! Run to receive the blessings of agency."  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2015/04/preserving-agency-protecting-religious-freedom?lang=eng

## 5. Jeffrey R. Holland

*Elder, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles (called 1994). Has spoken on religious freedom and the founding documents in multiple academic and interfaith contexts.*

---

**Title:** "A Promised Land"  
**Author:** Elder Jeffrey R. Holland  
**Date:** June 1976 (*Ensign*)  
**Context:** Essay published in the *Ensign*. Holland argues that the Lord prepared the conditions of American liberty — with "meticulous preparation and precise timing" — as the necessary precondition for the Restoration of the gospel. Cited in official 2026 Church curriculum on religious freedom.  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/ensign/1976/06/a-promised-land?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "Faith, Family, and Religious Freedom" (J. Reuben Clark Law Society)  
**Author:** Elder Jeffrey R. Holland, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** February 15, 2013  
**Context:** Address to the J. Reuben Clark Law Society. Addresses faith as the foundation of civil society, family as the basic social unit, and religious freedom as the protection for both. References Dostoevsky's Grand Inquisitor, Charles Taylor, and constitutional scholar Michael McConnell. Expanded and re-delivered at Chapman University in 2015.  
**Link (cited in Church resources):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/religious-freedom/teachings-from-church-leaders?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "Faith, Family, and Religious Freedom" (Chapman University)  
**Author:** Elder Jeffrey R. Holland, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** February 26, 2015  
**Context:** Public address at Chapman University, Orange, California. Expanded version of the 2013 J. Reuben Clark Law Society address. Discusses Clayton Christensen's well-known conversation with a Marxist economist from China about the role of religion in sustaining democracy. Includes extended reflection on Dostoevsky's *Brothers Karamazov* and the tension between liberty and security.  
**Link (full transcript):** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/full-transcript--elder-jeffrey-rholland-speaks-on-faith--family-and-religious-freedom-at-chapman-university  
**Link (Church prophets page):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/prophets-and-apostles/unto-all-the-world/faith-family-and-religious-freedom?lang=eng

---

**Title:** Address to New York LDS Professionals Association (Interfaith Religious Liberty)  
**Author:** Elder Jeffrey R. Holland, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** October 13, 2016  
**Context:** Address to the New York LDS Professionals Association. Discusses LDS–Catholic interfaith collaboration on religious liberty, praising Cardinal Timothy Dolan's advocacy. Covers the "Russell Amendment" protecting religious hiring, practical civic engagement, and the importance of interfaith coalitions.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/transcript-elder-holland-address-new-york-lds-professionals-association

## 6. David O. McKay

*President of the Church (1951–1970). Frequently addressed threats to freedom from communism and the importance of moral agency as the foundation of liberty. His most quoted line on the Constitution: "Next to being one in worshiping God, there is nothing in this world upon which this Church should be more united than in upholding and defending the Constitution of the United States" (Conference Report, October 1939).*

---

**Title:** Untitled Address / Constitutional Statement  
**Author:** President David O. McKay (then Second Counselor in the First Presidency)  
**Date:** October 1939 General Conference  
**Context:** General Conference remarks containing the most-quoted McKay statement on the Constitution: "Next to being one in worshiping God, there is nothing in this world upon which this Church should be more united than in upholding and defending the Constitution of the United States." Also found in *Statements on Communism and the Constitution of the United States* (Church publication). Reprinted in L. Tom Perry's BYU address "Truth and Liberty" (1987).  
**Link (primary source archive):** https://befriendtheconstitution.org/1939/10/06/untitled-address-president-david-o-mckay-2/  
**Link (RSC compilation of McKay quotes):** https://rsc.byu.edu/latter-day-prophets-united-states-constitution/david-o-mckay  
**Link (cited in Perry BYU address):** https://speeches.byu.edu/talks/l-tom-perry/truth-liberty/

---

**Title:** 1936 First Presidency Statement on Communism *(co-signed as Second Counselor)*  
**Author:** First Presidency: Heber J. Grant, J. Reuben Clark Jr., David O. McKay  
**Date:** July 3, 1936 (*Deseret News*)  
**Context:** Official First Presidency letter warning Church members against Communism as incompatible with constitutional government and the restored gospel. Declares: "Communism is not a political party nor a political plan under the Constitution; it is a system of government that is the opposite of our Constitutional government." Calls members to "completely eschew" Communism. One of the most consequential official statements on political liberty in LDS history. Quoted by Benson (1979) and others.  
**Link (quoted in full in Benson's 1979 General Conference address):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1979/10/a-witness-and-a-warning?lang=eng

---

**Title:** London Temple Dedicatory Prayer (excerpt on freedom)  
**Author:** President David O. McKay  
**Date:** September 7, 1958  
**Context:** Temple dedication prayer. McKay's prayer contains one of his most eloquent statements on freedom as a divine gift: "Next to life, we express gratitude for the gift of free agency. When thou didst create man, thou placed within him part of thine Omnipotence and bade him choose for himself. Liberty and conscience thus became a sacred part of human nature. Freedom not only to think, but to speak and act, is a God-given privilege." Quoted by Benson in his 1973 BYU address.  
**Link (quoted in Benson's "This Nation Shall Endure"):** https://speeches.byu.edu/talks/ezra-taft-benson/nation-shall-endure/  
**Link (original source — *Improvement Era*, October 1958, pp. 718–19):** Archive research required for original.

---

**Title:** "Agency and Responsibility" (compiled teachings)  
**Author:** President David O. McKay  
**Date:** Compiled 2011 (from various conference addresses)  
**Context:** Compilation of McKay's teachings on moral agency — which he called "God's greatest gift to man" — from across his ministry. Includes his argument that "Freedom of speech, freedom of action within boundaries that do not infringe upon the liberty of others are … divine gifts essential to human dignity and human happiness."  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/teachings-david-o-mckay/chapter-22?lang=eng

## 7. Dallin H. Oaks

*Elder, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles (1984–2018); First Counselor in the First Presidency (2018–2025); President of the Church (October 2025–present). Former Utah Supreme Court Justice, BYU Law School president, and law clerk to Chief Justice Earl Warren. In 2013, received the Becket Fund's Canterbury Medal for "courage in the defense of religious liberty." The single most prolific modern LDS voice on religious freedom and the Constitution.*

---

**Title:** "Religious Freedom" (BYU–Idaho Devotional)  
**Author:** Elder Dallin H. Oaks, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** October 13, 2009  
**Context:** Devotional at BYU–Idaho. Oaks calls the contest over religious freedom "a battle of eternal importance" and invites the rising generation to "march to the sound of the guns." Reviews constitutional history of the Free Exercise Clause, threats emerging from the Obama administration's language shift toward "freedom of worship" vs. "free exercise," and implications for Latter-day Saints. One of his first major addresses entirely devoted to this theme.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/oaks-religious-freedom

---

**Title:** "Fundamental Premises of Our Faith" (Harvard Law School)  
**Author:** Elder Dallin H. Oaks, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** February 2010  
**Context:** Academic address at Harvard Law School presenting the theological and constitutional foundations of LDS commitment to religious freedom to a legal academic audience.  
**Link (cited in Church resources):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/religious-freedom/teachings-from-church-leaders?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "Preserving Religious Freedom" / "People of Faith Should Defend Freedom of Religion" (Chapman University School of Law)  
**Author:** Elder Dallin H. Oaks, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** February 4, 2011  
**Context:** Academic address at Chapman University School of Law. Argues that religious freedom is the first and most foundational civil liberty in the American constitutional order. Reviews threats to the Free Exercise Clause, warns against narrowing the definition of religious expression, and addresses the conflict between religious liberty and LGBT civil rights claims.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/elder-oaks-religious-freedom-Chapman-University  
**Link (Church prophets page):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/prophets-and-apostles/unto-all-the-world/people-of-faith-should-defend-freedom-of-religion-elder-oaks-says?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "Fundamentals of Our Constitutions" (Utah Constitution Day Celebration)  
**Author:** Elder Dallin H. Oaks, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** September 17, 2010  
**Context:** Address at Utah's Constitution Day Celebration. Reviews divinely inspired constitutional fundamentals including separation of powers, Bill of Rights, and federalism.  
**Link (cited in Church resources):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/manual/religious-freedom/teachings-from-church-leaders?lang=eng

---

**Title:** "Hope for the Years Ahead" (Utah Valley University Constitutional Symposium on Religious Freedom)  
**Author:** Elder Dallin H. Oaks, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** April 16, 2014  
**Context:** Keynote address at UVU's Constitutional Symposium on Religious Freedom. Speaking "in behalf of all religious people," Oaks reviews threats to religious speech and free exercise, then argues for reasons for optimism, including growing legal advocacy organizations and university centers on law and religion. Identifies a healthy proliferation of academic religious freedom programs.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/transcript-elder-dallin-oaks-constitutional-symposium-religious-freedom

---

**Title:** "Loving Others and Living with Differences"  
**Author:** Elder Dallin H. Oaks, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** October 2014 General Conference  
**Context:** General Conference address. Addresses how Latter-day Saints can maintain firm convictions while treating those with different beliefs or lifestyles with genuine love and legal equality. Directly relevant to the balance between religious freedom and nondiscrimination principles.  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2014/10/loving-others-and-living-with-differences?lang=eng

---

**Title:** Regional Religious Freedom Conference, Dallas/Fort Worth  
**Author:** Elder Dallin H. Oaks, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** September 10, 2016  
**Context:** Address at the first-ever LDS Regional Religious Freedom Conference (Colleyville Stake Center, Texas, serving 25 stakes). Oaks reviews the history of declining Free Exercise protections, the narrowing of religious freedom to private worship only, and a "fairness for all" approach to navigating conflicts with nondiscrimination law. Joined by Elder Lance B. Wickman and Elder Von G. Keetch.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/transcript-elder-dallin-h-oaks-religious-freedom-dallas

---

**Title:** "Elections, Hope, and Freedom" (BYU Devotional)  
**Author:** Elder Dallin H. Oaks, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** September 13, 2016  
**Context:** BYU devotional address in the lead-up to the 2016 U.S. presidential election. Oaks expresses concern about declining religious freedom and free speech on university campuses and in public life. States: "I am convinced that a worldwide tide is currently running against both religious freedom and its parallel freedoms of speech and assembly." Calls for civility in political discourse. Delivered 45 years after his first BYU devotional.  
**Link (BYU Speeches — full text):** https://speeches.byu.edu/talks/dallin-h-oaks/elections-hope-freedom/  
**Link (Newsroom summary):** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/elder-oaks-addresses-elections-hope-freedom-byu

---

**Title:** "Defending Our Divinely Inspired Constitution"  
**Author:** President Dallin H. Oaks, First Counselor in the First Presidency  
**Date:** April 2021 General Conference  
**Context:** General Conference address — one of the most comprehensive modern LDS treatments of the U.S. Constitution. Identifies five divinely inspired constitutional elements: (1) separation of powers, (2) the Bill of Rights (especially First Amendment religious freedom), (3) federalism, (4) popular sovereignty, and (5) constitutional supremacy over individuals. Calls Latter-day Saints worldwide to a "unique responsibility" to uphold and defend the Constitution. Warns against trivializing the Constitution as a "loyalty test or political slogan."  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/2021/04/51oaks?lang=eng

**Title:** 2021 Joseph Smith Lecture on Religious Liberty, University of Virginia  
**Author:** President Dallin H. Oaks, First Counselor in the First Presidency  
**Date:** November 12, 2021  
**Context:** Delivered at the University of Virginia's Rotunda — the seventh Joseph Smith Lecture on religious liberty, described as a "landmark address in a landmark location." Oaks proposes a new workable balance between religious freedom and nondiscrimination — not litigation but mutual respect and good-faith negotiation. References the 1904–1907 Reed Smoot Senate hearings as a model. Described as the most difficult address he had ever given.

## 8. Ronald A. Rasband

*Elder, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles (called 2015). Delivered the most recent General Conference address devoted primarily to religious freedom.*

---

**Title:** "To Heal the World"  
**Author:** Elder Ronald A. Rasband, Quorum of the Twelve Apostles  
**Date:** April 2022 General Conference (Easter Sunday session)  
**Context:** General Conference address — the second consecutive Easter-Sunday General Conference address focused on religious freedom (following Hales in 2015). Identifies "attacks on religious freedom" as a global scourge. Outlines four benefits of religious freedom to society: (1) it protects individual conscience and agency; (2) it fosters expressions of belief, hope, and peace; (3) it enables good works and charitable service; and (4) it honors God. Reports on his participation in the G20 Interfaith Forum in Italy. Quotes Joseph Smith's declaration to defend the religious rights of all denominations.

## 9. Historical Church Statements & First Presidency Letters

*Official statements with broad institutional authority.*

---

**Title:** Doctrine and Covenants 134 — "Declaration of Belief Regarding Governments and Laws"  
**Date:** August 1835 (adopted at a general assembly of the Church, Kirtland, Ohio)  
**Context:** Official declaration adopted by the early Church, included as scriptural canon. Articles 2 and 4 are the foundational LDS scriptural statements on religious freedom: governments must "secure to each individual the free exercise of conscience," and civil law has "no right to interfere in prescribing rules of worship to bind the consciences of men." Remains part of the Doctrine and Covenants.  
**Link:** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/scriptures/dc-testament/dc/134?lang=eng

---

**Title:** First Presidency Statement on Communism  
**Author:** President Heber J. Grant; First Counselor J. Reuben Clark Jr.; Second Counselor David O. McKay  
**Date:** July 3, 1936 (*Deseret News*)  
**Context:** Official institutional statement warning that Communism "is a system of government that is the opposite of our Constitutional government" and that supporting it is "treasonable to our free institutions." Calls all Church members to "completely eschew" Communism. Quoted by multiple apostles as authoritative institutional position. One of the clearest First Presidency statements on liberty in Church history.  
**Link (quoted in full in Benson 1979):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1979/10/a-witness-and-a-warning?lang=eng

---

**Title:** Selected Beliefs and Statements on Religious Freedom (Official Church Compilation)  
**Date:** Compiled February 2011 (official Church Newsroom document)  
**Context:** Compiles statements on religious liberty from Joseph Smith, D&C 134, James E. Talmage (1899), J. Reuben Clark Jr. (1935), and other Church leaders. A useful orientation document for understanding the institutional tradition.  
**Link:** https://newsroom.churchofjesuschrist.org/article/beliefs-statements-religious-freedom

## 10. Supplemental: J. Reuben Clark Jr.

*First Counselor in the First Presidency under Presidents Heber J. Grant, George Albert Smith, and David O. McKay (1933–1945; 1951–1961). Renowned constitutional scholar, former U.S. Ambassador to Mexico, and Under Secretary of State. Although serving in the First Presidency rather than the Quorum of the Twelve, his statements are quasi-official and are among the most frequently quoted on liberty and the Constitution in LDS discourse.*

---

**Title:** *Stand Fast by Our Constitution*  
**Author:** President J. Reuben Clark Jr., First Counselor in the First Presidency  
**Date:** Compiled from addresses; published 1962 by Deseret Book  
**Context:** Collection of Clark's writings and addresses on the U.S. Constitution, sovereignty, and liberty. Contains the celebrated warning: "The price of liberty is and always has been blood, human blood, and if our liberties are lost, we shall never regain them except at the price of blood. They must not be lost!" Also includes his declaration: "The Constitution of the United States is a great and treasured part of my religion." Quoted by Ezra Taft Benson and Dallin Oaks repeatedly.  
**Link (quotes referenced in Benson 1976 General Conference):** https://www.churchofjesuschrist.org/study/general-conference/1976/04/the-constitution-a-glorious-standard?lang=eng  
**Note:** The book itself is out of print; available through Deseret Book archives or libraries.

---

**Title:** "Constitution and Its Signers" (BYU Address)  
**Author:** President J. Reuben Clark Jr.  
**Date:** May 21, 1957 (BYU)  
**Context:** Address at Brigham Young University arguing that the U.S. Constitution is "an inspired document penned by remarkable men." Audio is available through BYU Speeches; text is not separately published.  
**Link (BYU Speeches — audio):** https://speeches.byu.edu/talks/j-reuben-clark/constitution/
